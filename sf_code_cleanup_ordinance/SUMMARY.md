# Plain-language summary of changes — "Leg Ver3,4"

**Document:** San Francisco Board of Supervisors ordinance (sponsors: Mandelman; Dorsey), 362 pages.
**Official title:** *[Various Codes – Streamlining Reporting Requirements and Procedures, Removing Obsolete Programs and Requirements, and Other Code Cleanup]* — an omnibus ordinance amending the Administrative, Environment, Health, Labor & Employment, Park, Planning, Police, Public Works, Subdivision, Transportation, and Building Inspection Commission Codes.

## The big picture
This is overwhelmingly a **deletion / cleanup** ordinance, not an expansion.

- **~558,000 characters struck (deleted) vs. ~25,000 added** — roughly a 22:1 ratio.
- Two things are happening: (1) **whole obsolete chapters and programs are repealed**, and (2) **numerous reporting requirements are trimmed or consolidated** (typically many/frequent reports → a single annual report).

## Large changes (wholesale repeals of entire chapters/programs)

| Area | What it is | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Chapter 12D** (~203K chars deleted — by far the largest) | The Minority/Women/Local Business Enterprise (MBE/WBE/LBE) Utilization Ordinance, including its enormous "General Findings" and "Additional Findings Supporting Race- and Gender-Conscious Bid Preferences." | **Repealed in full.** Long-superseded findings and program text removed. |
| **Chapter 14A** (~59K) | Disadvantaged/Local Business Enterprise & non-discrimination-in-contracting ordinance (findings, powers & duties of the Controller and contract-awarding authorities). | **Repealed in full.** Superseded contracting scheme removed. |
| **Chapter 10E** (~51K deleted / 13K added) | Housing & area-plan monitoring and reporting (Downtown Plan, Eastern Neighborhoods, Balboa Park Station, Housing Element production reports). | Mixed: several monitoring programs deleted; the annual housing reporting requirement is **rewritten/consolidated**. |
| **Chapter 10** (~30K deleted / 4K added) | Assorted administrative funds and their reporting duties. | Mostly deletions with a few rewrites (see medium changes). |
| **Ch. 341** (~11K) | Better Neighborhoods Area Plan Monitoring Program (annual + time-series reports). | Repealed. |
| **Ch. 84** (~10K) | QHWRA Disqualification Rent Assistance Program. | Repealed (program obsolete). |
| **Ch. 103** (~9.5K) | Housing Balance Monitoring and Reporting. | Repealed. |
| **Ch. 10C** (~8.6K) | Tow-reimbursement/waiver program and Chief of Police reporting on it. | Repealed. |
| **Ch. 97** (~7.6K) | Healthcare Impact Reports program. | Repealed. |
| **Ch. 92** (~6.3K) | Pilot Consumer Loan Education & Counseling Program. | Repealed (pilot concluded). |
| **Ch. 206** (~4K) | Affordable Housing Bonus Program evaluation/reporting. | Repealed. |

**Takeaway:** the bulk of the page count is removing legally obsolete findings and expired/superseded programs — especially the old race- and gender-conscious contracting chapters (12D, 14A) and a series of one-time or pilot programs whose reporting mandates no longer serve a purpose.

## Medium changes (substantive substitutions — how agencies report changes)

These are the ~16 places where text is both deleted **and** replaced, i.e. real policy edits rather than pure repeals. The recurring pattern is **reducing reporting frequency and consolidating standalone reports into existing annual reports.**

- **Police / Airport staffing (p. 9):** Removes the fixed baseline of a **"mandated level of 1,971 officers"** and the detailed staffing-needs methodology reporting; replaces it with the **"recommended staffing level as described in Section 4.127."**
- **Sexual Harassment Policy — DHR (§16.9-25, p. 250):** Changes the Human Resources Director from filing **quarterly** sexual-harassment-complaint reports to the Commission on the Status of Women, to a single **annual** report to the Mayor and Board of Supervisors.
- **Disability & Aging Services community-living fund (§10.100-53, p. 34):** Replaces a **semi-annual** reporting requirement with an **annual plan** submitted to the Board.
- **Administrative Services vehicle-leasing program (§10.100-9, p. 28):** Drops standalone **semi-annual** reports; the vehicle number/type/cost information now rides along with the department's **annual budget submission.**
- **Film Production Fund (§10.100-297, p. 46):** Eliminates a separate March-1 annual report; the information is folded into the Film Commission's **existing Annual Report** (Admin Code §57.8).
- **Area-plan impact fees (p. 264):** Shifts to **annual** reporting on impact fees collected and how they were allocated/spent under the Community Improvements Plan.
- **Sexual harassment cross-reference (§16.9-27, p. 252):** Clarifies that the DHR annual-report duty under §16.9-25(e) still applies (housekeeping edit).

## Caveats about this parse (read before relying on specifics)
- **Front matter, page 3:** shows many add/delete marks, but these are just the **renumbering of the title's enumerated digest list** (items 1…110+), not substantive changes — ignore.
- Marks are detected **per word**: a word fully covered by a mid-height bar = *deleted*, a bottom bar = *added*. Validated against sample pages (clean separation). Rare edge cases (a word half-struck, or punctuation attached to a word) may be tagged coarsely.
- Reading order is reconstructed geometrically; the document is single-column, so order is reliable. Page headers/footers and the 1–25 margin line numbers were stripped.
- Character counts are approximate proxies for "size," useful for ranking, not exact legal word counts.
