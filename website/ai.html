About AI

AI starts with your data

AI is an incredibly powerful tool. But it is only as powerful as the information you give it.

The Foundation

Are you AI ready?

They say "garbage in, garbage out." That has never been more true than with AI.

AI needs context to be useful. It needs to know what each database, table, and column is and how they relate to one another. Even individual values need explanation. No AI is going to correctly interpret "Type = 2" without someone documenting what that means.

This is metadata: data about your data. It is the foundation that AI reads to understand your systems. Without it, you get confident answers built on bad assumptions. With it, you get something that actually works.

Getting AI ready is not about buying a new platform. It is about documenting what you have, cleaning up what is broken, and making sure your data tells the whole story.

The Human Element

Can AI do your job?

Definitely some of it. But probably not the part you are worried about.

If everything in your system is mapped source to target, fully documented, and well understood, then congratulations — AI can probably take it from here, and you probably do not need us.

But that is rarely the case. Most organizations run on institutional knowledge: the things your senior analyst knows that have never been written down. The context behind why a report excludes certain records. The business logic that lives in someone's head and not in the codebase.

We help bridge that gap. We connect the human side — the knowledge your team carries — to the technical side, so that both you and the AI have the whole picture. The goal is not to replace your team. It is to make sure the tools they use actually understand what they are working with.
