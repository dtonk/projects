# Street Tree Maintenance

Street trees in the city are maintained by the Department of Public Works' Bureau of Urban Forestry. Residents can report issues with street trees through 311.

Common reportable issues include: dead or dying trees, fallen branches blocking sidewalks or streets, trees damaged in storms, branches interfering with power lines (these are referred to PG&E), trees lifting sidewalks, and overhanging branches blocking street signs or traffic signals.

For trees that pose an immediate hazard — fully fallen across a road, branches actively breaking, trees leaning on structures — call 311 and clearly state it's an emergency, or call 911 if the situation is life-threatening. Emergency tree removal is typically dispatched within 4 hours.

Routine tree pruning is performed on a rotating maintenance schedule, neighborhood by neighborhood. The Bureau of Urban Forestry generally prunes each street tree once every 3-5 years. Requests for out-of-cycle pruning are evaluated based on safety and accessibility, not aesthetics.

To plant a new street tree, residents can apply through the Friends of the Urban Forest program or request a tree from the city's tree-planting program. There is no cost to the resident, but the property owner becomes responsible for ongoing watering during the first 3 years of establishment.

Removing a healthy street tree (even one on your own property's frontage) requires a permit. Unauthorized removal can result in fines of $1,000-$5,000.
