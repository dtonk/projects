# Street Sign Repair and Replacement

Street signs — including stop signs, speed limit signs, street name signs, and regulatory signs — are maintained by the San Francisco Department of Public Works (DPW).

Report damaged, missing, faded, or knocked-down signs through 311. Include the exact intersection or block location and a description of which sign is affected.

Response times by priority:
- Missing or knocked-down stop signs: treated as a traffic safety emergency, typically replaced within 24-48 hours.
- Missing street name signs: repaired within 5-10 business days.
- Faded or damaged (but readable) signs: repaired within 30 days.

If a stop sign is completely missing or knocked down, you can also call DPW's 24-hour emergency line at 415-695-2020 to expedite the response.

Do not attempt to remove, reposition, or temporarily replace a sign yourself — unauthorized sign modification is illegal and can create liability.

Traffic signal issues (broken lights, missing signals, signals not functioning) are handled by the SFMTA Signal Shop. Report these through 311 or directly to SFMTA at 415-553-9960. A dark or flashing red traffic signal should be treated as a 4-way stop.

Temporary construction signs blocking or obscuring permanent signs should be reported to DPW. Contractors are required to maintain sign visibility during work.

Pedestrian walk/don't walk signals and countdown timers are also SFMTA's responsibility. Report issues through 311 or the SFMTA site.
