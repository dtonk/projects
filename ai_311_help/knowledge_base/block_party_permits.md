# Block Party and Street Closure Permits

Residents can apply for a street closure permit to hold block parties, community events, festivals, or neighborhood gatherings on their street.

The permit application goes through the San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency (SFMTA) for street closures, and may also require coordination with the Fire Department, Police Department, and DPW depending on the size and nature of the event.

How to apply:
1. Submit a Special Event Permit application through the City's Special Events Office (part of the Mayor's Office) at least 30 days before the event (60+ days recommended for larger events).
2. For simple neighborhood block parties, you can apply directly through SFMTA with fewer requirements — typically 2–4 weeks lead time.
3. You'll need a list of affected addresses (to notify neighbors) and a basic event description.

Block parties on residential streets that close a single block typically cost $100–$200 in permit fees and require proof of neighbors' agreement. The city may waive or reduce fees for community-building events in lower-income neighborhoods.

Noise amplification (speakers, live music) at events requires a separate Sound Amplification Permit from the Entertainment Commission, issued at no cost for most neighborhood events.

Food served to the public may require a temporary food facility permit from the SF Department of Public Health. Nonprofits and community groups are often exempt for small gatherings.

If you see a street closure without visible permits or traffic control, or a street party that is blocking emergency vehicle access, report it through 311 or call 911 if there is an emergency access issue.
