# Sidewalk Repair

In this city, sidewalk maintenance is the responsibility of the adjacent property owner, not the city. This applies to both residential and commercial properties.

If you notice a damaged sidewalk — cracks wider than half an inch, uplifted concrete sections creating trip hazards, large potholes in the sidewalk surface, or sidewalks damaged by tree roots — you can report it through 311. The Department of Public Works (DPW) will inspect and notify the property owner if repair is required.

Property owners have 30 days from the date of the official repair notice to either complete repairs or apply for a sidewalk repair permit. Failure to comply can result in the city completing the repair and billing the property owner, plus an administrative fee.

The city offers a sidewalk repair subsidy program for low-income property owners. Eligibility is based on household income, and the program can cover up to 100% of repair costs for qualifying applicants.

For sidewalks damaged by city-owned street trees, property owners may be eligible for partial cost-sharing through the Bureau of Urban Forestry. Submit a request for joint repair through 311.

If you trip and are injured on a damaged sidewalk, you may file a claim with the City Attorney's Office. Liability typically rests with the property owner, but the city may share responsibility in certain situations involving public infrastructure like streetlights or fire hydrants.
