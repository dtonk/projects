# Homeless Services Requests

To request outreach for a person experiencing homelessness, file a request through 311. The request will be routed to the Healthy Streets Operations Center (HSOC), which coordinates outreach teams.

Provide as much detail as you can: location, description of the person, any visible signs of medical distress, and whether the person has been at the location for an extended period. Photos are helpful but please respect the dignity and privacy of the individual.

Outreach teams typically respond within 24-72 hours depending on caseload. They offer voluntary services including shelter referrals, medical assessment, behavioral health support, and case management. Outreach is not enforcement — teams cannot force anyone to accept services or move.

For medical emergencies (someone unresponsive, in severe distress, or in immediate danger), call 911. Do not file a 311 ticket in place of emergency services.

To report an encampment of multiple tents or structures, file an "encampment" 311 ticket with the address or cross-streets. Encampment response involves outreach first; cleanup is scheduled afterward.

Donations and volunteer opportunities are coordinated through nonprofit partners like Glide, St. Anthony's, and the Homeless Outreach Team (HOT). The city's Department of Homelessness and Supportive Housing maintains a directory of partner organizations.

Personal property left behind during outreach is stored at the city's bag-and-tag facility for 90 days, where the owner can retrieve it.
