# Storm Drains and Flooding

The city operates over 25,000 catch basins (storm drains) that capture rainwater from streets and direct it to the sewer system. The SF Public Utilities Commission (SFPUC) is responsible for maintenance.

Report clogged storm drains through 311. Common causes of clogs include leaves, trash, sediment, and grease. Standard cleanout requests are typically completed within 5 business days, but ahead of major storms the city pre-clears known problem drains.

During heavy rain, if water is pooling in the street or rising toward homes:
- For pooling that's not immediately threatening property, file a 311 ticket with the location.
- For active flooding into a home, garage, or basement, this is an emergency — call 311 and clearly state it's an active flood, or call 911 if there's immediate danger.

The Adopt-a-Drain program lets residents take responsibility for keeping a nearby drain clear. Volunteers receive a free "drain kit" with broom and bag, and can name their adopted drain on the city's public map. This significantly reduces flooding during rainstorms.

Sewer backups inside a home (raw sewage coming up through drains, toilets, or basement floors) should be reported immediately to SFPUC at 415-695-7300. Backups caused by the city's main sewer line may qualify for cleanup and damage reimbursement.

If a manhole cover is missing, displaced, or damaged, treat it as a hazard. Report immediately and warn other pedestrians/drivers if possible. The city responds to missing manhole covers within 4 hours.

Residents are responsible for keeping the catch basin in front of their property clear of leaves and debris, especially during storm season (October through April).
