# Construction Permits and Complaints

Building permits in San Francisco are issued by the Department of Building Inspection (DBI). Most construction, renovation, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical work requires a permit.

To check if work near you is permitted, visit the DBI permit portal at sfdbi.org and search by address. Permit records are public.

To report unpermitted construction work:
- File a complaint through 311 or directly with DBI at 415-558-6088.
- DBI inspectors investigate complaints and can issue stop-work orders and fines.
- Common violations: building without a permit, work exceeding the scope of an issued permit, occupied units with ongoing construction.

Construction noise hours: Permitted construction noise is allowed Monday–Friday 7am–8pm, and Saturday 8am–6pm. Construction is prohibited on Sundays and holidays unless a special noise variance is obtained. Violations should be reported to DBI or the SF Police Department's non-emergency line.

Right-of-way encroachments (construction dumpsters or containers in the street, scaffolding over sidewalks) require permits from DPW. If scaffolding or a construction dumpster is blocking the sidewalk or street without a visible permit, report it through 311.

Sidewalk closure during construction requires a pedestrian protection plan. The contractor must maintain a safe path of travel (at least 36 inches wide) and proper signage. If a sidewalk is fully blocked with no accessible alternative, file a 311 complaint.

Contractors who cause damage to public infrastructure (street, sidewalk, curb) during construction are liable for repairs. Report damage to DPW via 311.
