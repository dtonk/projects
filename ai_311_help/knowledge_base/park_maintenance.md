# Park Maintenance and Issues

San Francisco Recreation and Parks (Rec & Park) manages over 220 parks, squares, playgrounds, and green spaces across the city.

Report park maintenance issues through 311 or directly through the Rec & Park website. Common issues include:
- Broken playground equipment
- Damaged benches, tables, or fencing
- Downed trees or branches in parks
- Overgrown vegetation
- Vandalism or graffiti in parks
- Broken irrigation or flooding
- Restroom maintenance issues

For playground equipment that poses an immediate injury risk (sharp edges, structural failure, fall hazard), note "safety hazard" in your 311 report so it is escalated. Standard maintenance requests are typically addressed within 5-10 business days.

Dog off-leash hours: Most parks allow dogs off-leash in designated areas during specific hours, typically early morning (before 9am) and evening (after 5pm). Check the Rec & Park website for your specific park's rules. Violations can be reported to Animal Care and Control at 415-554-9400.

Permitted reservations for picnic areas, athletic fields, and event spaces are managed through Rec & Park's online reservation system. Conflicts with unpermitted use of a reserved area should be reported to park staff or 311.

Park rangers patrol city parks and can be reached through 311. For emergencies in parks — assault, medical emergency, fire — call 911.

The Golden Gate Park Concourse, Ocean Beach, and Crissy Field fall under different jurisdictional overlaps (city, state, federal). For issues in those areas, reporting through 311 will route to the appropriate agency.
