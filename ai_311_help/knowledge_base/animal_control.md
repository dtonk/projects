# Animal Control Services

Animal Care and Control (ACC) handles animal-related issues in the city, from lost pets to wildlife encounters to dangerous animals.

Report through 311 or call ACC directly at 415-554-9400 for:
- Loose or stray dogs and cats
- Injured or sick stray animals
- Dangerous or aggressive animals
- Animal bites (also call 911 or seek medical care first if you've been bitten)
- Animal cruelty or neglect concerns
- Dead animals on public streets (pickup is free and usually completed within 24 hours)
- Wildlife in distress (raccoons, opossums, coyotes, birds)

For immediate emergencies — an aggressive dog actively threatening people, a wild animal trapped inside a home, severe injury to an animal — call 911 or ACC's emergency line. Standard 311 reports are not treated as emergencies.

The city is home to coyotes, particularly in larger parks. Coyote sightings alone are not emergencies; coyotes generally avoid humans. Report aggressive coyote behavior (approaching people, following, growling) so ACC can investigate. Never feed coyotes, as habituation increases dangerous interactions.

Lost pets can be searched for at the ACC shelter at 1419 Bryant Street. Strays are typically held for at least 4-7 days before becoming available for adoption. If your pet is microchipped and registered, you may be contacted directly.

ACC also offers low-cost vaccination, microchipping, and spay/neuter services for pets of low-income residents.

Animal licensing is required for dogs over 4 months old. Licenses help reunite lost dogs with owners and fund ACC services.
