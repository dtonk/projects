# Illegal Dumping

Illegal dumping — leaving trash, furniture, construction debris, or hazardous materials on public streets, sidewalks, alleys, vacant lots, or in parks — is a crime in this city. Fines range from $500 to $1,500 per incident, with higher penalties for hazardous waste or repeat offenders.

To report illegal dumping, file a 311 ticket with the exact location and description of the items. If you witnessed the dumping, include any vehicle description and license plate (only if it's safe to gather this information — do not confront dumpers).

Public Works crews typically clean up reported dumping within 48-72 hours. Hazardous materials (paint, oil, chemicals, syringes) are handled by a separate hazmat team and may take slightly longer for safe removal.

Common dumping hotspots include alleys, freeway underpasses, dead-end streets, and areas behind commercial properties. The city has installed cameras at some chronic dumping sites; footage is used to identify and prosecute offenders.

If items are dumped on private property, the property owner is responsible for cleanup. The city can issue a notice requiring cleanup within 30 days; failure to comply may result in city cleanup at the owner's expense plus an administrative fee.

Construction and demolition debris from contractors must be hauled to a licensed facility. Contractors caught illegally dumping face higher fines and possible loss of business permits.

To dispose of large quantities of waste legally, residents can use the Tunnel Avenue Resource Recovery Facility (transfer station). Drop-off fees apply but are far less than illegal dumping fines.

Rewards of up to $1,000 may be offered for tips leading to conviction of illegal dumpers.
