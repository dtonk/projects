# Graffiti Removal

The city removes graffiti from public property at no charge. For graffiti on private property, the city provides free graffiti removal to property owners who sign a one-time consent form authorizing the city to enter the property for cleanup purposes.

Report graffiti through 311 with the address and a photo if possible. Graffiti containing hate speech, racial slurs, or threats is treated as a priority and typically removed within 48 hours.

Standard graffiti on public infrastructure (sidewalks, mailboxes, utility boxes, public buildings, transit shelters) is removed within 7-14 business days depending on volume.

Property owners who have not signed the consent form will receive a notice giving them 30 days to remove graffiti themselves. Failure to remove graffiti after the notice may result in a fine and the city completing the removal at the owner's expense.

If you witness someone in the act of creating graffiti, do not confront them. Call 911 if it's in progress or the non-emergency police line at 415-553-0123 to file a report. Graffiti vandalism is a misdemeanor and can be a felony if damages exceed $400.

The city does not remove graffiti from privately-owned vehicles. That's the responsibility of the vehicle owner.
