# Streetlight Outages

The city operates approximately 44,000 streetlights, maintained by the SF Public Utilities Commission (SFPUC) in partnership with PG&E for power-related issues.

Report a streetlight outage through 311 with the pole number (printed on a small tag attached to most light poles, usually facing the street) or the nearest address. If you can't find the pole number, the address and a brief description of the light's location is sufficient.

Standard streetlight repairs are completed within 10 business days. Outages in high-priority areas (intersections, school zones, areas with reported safety concerns) are addressed more quickly, typically within 5 business days.

If multiple lights are out on the same block, it usually indicates a circuit issue rather than individual bulb failure. These are flagged as higher priority and may require coordination with PG&E.

Lights that are dim, flickering, stuck on during the day, or shining into a residential window can also be reported through 311. Sensor adjustments and shielding are part of routine maintenance.

Decorative or pedestrian-scale lighting in parks is maintained by the Recreation and Parks Department, not SFPUC. 311 will route your report to the appropriate agency.

If a streetlight pole is damaged (knocked over, leaning, with exposed wiring), treat it as an electrical hazard. Call 911 or PG&E's emergency line at 1-800-743-5002 — do not approach the pole.
