# Parking Complaints and Violations

The Municipal Transportation Agency (SFMTA) handles parking enforcement. For active parking violations you'd like ticketed, call the parking enforcement line at 415-553-1200 — these requests are dispatched in real time, unlike 311 reports.

Common violations dispatched through parking enforcement:
- Blocked driveways
- Vehicles blocking fire hydrants
- Double-parked vehicles
- Vehicles parked in red zones, bus stops, or crosswalks
- Vehicles parked over the time limit in metered spaces
- Cars blocking handicapped ramps or accessible parking

For ongoing issues like a vehicle parked over street cleaning, or a vehicle that hasn't moved in days (potentially abandoned), file a 311 ticket. See the separate Abandoned Vehicles FAQ for the criteria.

Street sweeping schedules are posted on signs on every block. Tickets for street cleaning violations are typically $76-$92 depending on the area. The SF311 mobile app shows your current location's cleaning schedule.

If your vehicle has been towed, check the SFMTA tow lookup tool at sfmta.com/tow or call 415-553-1235. Vehicles are usually held at AutoReturn (450 7th Street). Recovery requires the registered owner's ID, current registration, and proof of insurance.

Tickets can be contested online within 21 days. You can request an "administrative review" first (no in-person hearing required), and if that's denied, escalate to a formal hearing.

Residential Permit Parking (RPP) area enforcement is handled by SFMTA. To apply for or renew a residential parking permit, visit the SFMTA permit office or apply online.
