# Water Leaks and Water Service Issues

The San Francisco Public Utilities Commission (SFPUC) manages the city's water distribution system, including all water mains and fire hydrants.

**Report a water main break or street leak immediately** by calling SFPUC's 24-hour emergency line at 415-695-7300. Do not wait to file a 311 ticket — active main breaks require immediate dispatch. Signs of a main break include water gushing from the street, sudden sinkholes or ground movement, or large amounts of water flowing down a street with no apparent source.

**For non-emergency leaks** (slow seepage, wet pavement, dripping fire hydrant, leaking water meter box), report through 311 or call SFPUC at 415-695-7300.

Water service inside your home (from the meter into the building) is the property owner's responsibility. The city is responsible for the water main up to and including the meter.

**High water bill / suspected leak:** If you receive an unusually high bill, it may indicate a silent leak (running toilet, dripping faucet, irrigation leak). SFPUC has a leak detection program and may offer free inspections for residential customers. Contact SFPUC customer service at 415-551-3000.

**Fire hydrant issues:** Damaged or leaking fire hydrants should be reported to SFPUC immediately. Never park within 15 feet of a fire hydrant — this is enforceable by SFMTA parking enforcement at any time.

**Flooding from broken pipes or hydrants** that is entering homes or creating a hazard on the street should be reported as an emergency. Call 415-695-7300 and, if the flooding poses immediate danger, also call 911.

Water conservation is required year-round in San Francisco. Report water waste (sprinklers running into the street, open hydrant caps not associated with city work) through 311.
