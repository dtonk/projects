# Reporting Potholes

Potholes on city streets are repaired by the Department of Public Works (DPW). To report a pothole, you'll need the exact location (nearest cross streets or street address) and a brief description of the size and depth.

Standard potholes on city-maintained streets are typically filled within 72 hours of being reported. Large or dangerous potholes that pose an immediate hazard to vehicles or pedestrians are prioritized and may be addressed within 24 hours.

Potholes on state highways (such as portions of US-101 or I-280 that pass through the city) should be reported to Caltrans, not 311. If you're unsure who maintains the street, report it through 311 and we'll route it appropriately.

There is no charge to the resident for pothole repair on public streets. Repairs to potholes on private property, in private parking lots, or on driveways are the responsibility of the property owner.

If a pothole damaged your vehicle, you can file a claim with the City Attorney's Office, but you'll need to show that the city had prior notice of the pothole and failed to repair it within a reasonable time. Filing a 311 report creates that record.
