# Abandoned Vehicles

A vehicle is considered abandoned if it has not moved for 72 consecutive hours on a public street, or shows signs of long-term neglect such as flat tires, expired registration over 6 months old, broken windows, or visible damage that prevents safe operation.

To report an abandoned vehicle, file a 311 ticket with the license plate, make, model, color, and street location. An officer from the Police Department's Abandoned Vehicle Detail will inspect the vehicle, typically within 3 business days.

If the vehicle meets the abandoned criteria, a warning notice is placed on the windshield giving the owner 72 hours to move it. If the vehicle remains in place after the notice period, it will be towed at the owner's expense.

Vehicles parked legally but that haven't moved are not necessarily abandoned. Residents are allowed to park on the street as long as the vehicle is operational, currently registered, and complies with street cleaning and parking signs.

For vehicles on private property (parking lots, driveways), the property owner is responsible for arranging towing through a private tow company. The city does not tow vehicles from private property except in specific safety situations.

Stolen or recovered stolen vehicles should be reported directly to the Police Department, not 311.
