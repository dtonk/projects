# Noise Complaints

The city's noise ordinance restricts loud noise that disturbs residents, especially between 10pm and 7am on weekdays and 11pm to 8am on weekends.

For ongoing noise issues like loud neighbors, barking dogs, or persistent construction outside permitted hours, file a noise complaint through 311. Complaints are routed to the appropriate agency: residential noise typically goes to the Police Department's non-emergency line, while commercial or construction noise goes to the Department of Building Inspection.

For noise happening right now that requires immediate response (a loud party, ongoing disturbance), call the Police non-emergency line directly at 415-553-0123 rather than filing a 311 ticket — 311 reports are not real-time dispatches.

Construction noise is generally permitted between 7am and 8pm Monday through Friday, and 9am to 8pm on weekends. Construction outside these hours requires a special after-hours permit. If you suspect a contractor is working without a permit, file a complaint with the address and approximate time of the noise.

Amplified music from bars, clubs, and entertainment venues is regulated by the Entertainment Commission. Repeated violations can result in venue fines and permit review.

You can file noise complaints anonymously, but providing your contact information helps inspectors follow up if they need more details.
